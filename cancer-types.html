Cancer Types

"Understanding Cancer: Know the Signs, Reduce the Risks, Take Control"

Lung Cancer

Lung cancer is common and deadly, often caused by smoking, though non-smokers can also be affected. It mostly occurs in people over 50, with symptoms like a persistent cough, chest pain, or shortness of breath. Family history, smoking, and conditions like COPD increase the risk.

Prostate Cancer

Prostate cancer affects men, especially those over 50. Symptoms may include difficulty urinating or blood in the urine. Family history and being overweight are key risk factors.

Breast Cancer

Breast cancer mainly affects women and becomes more common with age. Symptoms include a lump in the breast, changes in breast shape, or nipple discharge. Risk factors include being overweight, family history, and hormone-related conditions.

Ovarian Cancer

Ovarian cancer is hard to detect early, often diagnosed in women over 50. Common signs include bloating, pelvic pain, and feeling full quickly. Family history, obesity, and smoking increase the risk.

Bowel Cancer

Bowel cancer (Colorectal Cancer)  typically affects people over 50 and presents with symptoms like blood in stool, persistent stomach pain, or changes in bowel habits. Obesity, smoking, and a family history of the disease increase the risk.
